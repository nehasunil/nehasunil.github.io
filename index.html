About Me

I’m a graduate student at MIT doing research in autonomous robotic manipulation. I received my bachelor's degree from Caltech in mechanical engineering (’19). I have an interdisciplinary background and an interest in medical robotics. I'd love to be a part of the conceptual and developmental stages of the next game-changing technology. I want to be challenged to think creatively and push boundaries, all while changing lives for the better.

Skills

Robotics

Worked at Verb Surgical. Coursework in manipulator kinematics, motion planning, autonomy, and basic control theory. Projects involved computer vision, haptics, and ROS.

Programming

Coding since middle school. Experienced in Python, Java, C, C++, R, VB, MATLAB, Mathematica, HTML, and Arduino. Used GitHub for version control.

Computer Vision

Implemented computer vision using SimpleCV (Python wrapper for OpenCV) for autonomous movement to goal. Additional experience with sci-kit image.

Mechatronics

Experience with sensors, actuators, & control systems in lab course and at Verb Surgical. Designed & debugged circuits. Responsible for transmission subsystem in robotics course.

Computer-Aided Design

Finite Element Analysis

Classroom and research experience with Finite Element Analysis and Computational Fluid Dynamics using ANSYS and COMSOL. Teaching Assistant for undergraduate course.

Biological Research

Data Analysis

Problem Solving
